Antiquity

Shield Composition

Early shields were made from natural materials (wood, leather). They were mainly used to protect against physical attacks.

Symbolic Value

In ancient societies (Greeks, Romans), the shield symbolized collective defense and belonging to a city or army. For example, among Greek hoplites, losing oneâ€™s shield was considered dishonorable.

Middle Ages

Shield Composition

Medieval shields were often made of metal and came in larger forms (kite shield, escutcheon). They featured heraldic motifs representing noble families and knights.

Symbolic Value

The shield became a symbol of nobility, loyalty, and social identity. The coat of arms adorning the shields was a sign of prestige and family status.

Renaissance and Modern Era

Role of the Shield

From the Renaissance onward, shields gradually lost their usefulness on battlefields with the emergence of firearms. They were still made of metal and often used for specific tactics.

Symbolic Value

The shield remains a symbol of protection today, but its usage has become limited, losing part of its social symbolism to modern weaponry.

Contemporary Era

Role of the Shield

Today, shields are mainly used by law enforcement (riot shields) or as ballistic protection (bulletproof shields). They are made of transparent polycarbonate, metal, and woodâ€”a blend of materials.

Symbolic Value

In a contemporary context, the shield represents public order protection and peacekeeping, serving as a defense tool in modern conflicts.
